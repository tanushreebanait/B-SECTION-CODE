A.P.J Abdul Kalam

Theoretical Physicist & Nobel Laureate

He was born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, on October 15th, 1931. Kalam had been selling newspapers to support the income of his family. In 1960, he studied aeronautical engineering at the Madras Institute of Technology. His dream as a child was to become a fighter pilot.

Major Achievements

"Missile Man of India": His work on developing ballistic missile and launch vehicle technology earned him the title "Missile Man of India". Nuclear Tests: He played a key role in the Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998, which established India as a nuclear power. Chief Scientific Advisor: He served as the Chief Scientific Advisor to the Prime Minister and the Secretary of the DRDO from 1992 to 1999.
